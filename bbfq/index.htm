Intro

With no January BBFQ and so no soundtracks to memorise, I thought I'd take a look at the history of previous results, all available on the BBFQ website.

Some statistics from the last four years of quizzes:

  • 126 Quizzes
  • 2,735 Teams
  • 121,431.25 Points
  • 67.5 points Highest Score
  • 1 point Lowest Score
  • 12.5 points Biggest Winning Margin

The Teams

A total of 2,735 teams took part in 126 quizzes. North London generally has the biggest quizzes, averaging 25 teams (vs. an overall average of 22). There's a bit of an uptick in North London after 2013, which I guess corresponds to the move to the Assembly House.

Team Names

Of the 2,735 teams, there are 1,776 different team names. Obviously this still masks a lot of slightly different spellings, typos etc. I had a crack at grouping teams that appeared more five or more times into 'franchises' – appreciating this is really subjective (see notes). There are 74 franchises which together make up about 40% of all teams. V for Vienetta have appeared the most times (41).

Teams aren't afraid of a bit of bad language when it comes to picking names. In the list of team names we have: 31 fucks, 28 shits, 5 pisses, 3 wankers and 2 c*nts. There were 364 mentions of 'quiz'.

Scores

What does it take to win?

At least 52.5 points – that was the lowest winning score. On average, teams need to be scoring 60 or more (out of 70) to win. East London and South London are quite similar, but it's a little harder to win in North London where the average winning score is 62.

Average and winning scores are remarkably consistent. There's relatively little variation over time (so the quiz hasn't gotten easier or more difficult), or across venues.

Notes

The data was taken from each of the results pages ('random' example) on the BBFQ website. You can see the python script for scraping the data and the raw data itself, here. Charts are created with plotly.

Results were available from Jan 2012 (North London) or Apr 2012 (South & East London) onwards. The old website had some results 2005-2012, but I founf the formats a bit variable so haven't included them in this page.

To assign the 'franchises' I had to make some educated guesses — my basic criteria was any team name that appeared five or more times in the data. I combined derived versions where they were obvious, e.g. Analrapists and Christmas with the Analrapists. I ignored some generic 'quiz' names like Quizteam Aguilera as I couldn't be sure they related to the same team. I'm sure there're plenty of teams who use very different team names each month, but I had no way of identifying them.
